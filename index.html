Bharat Sharma

Taking Dance to its Audience...More..

Taking Dance to its Audience...

close

Relationship between dance and its audience is cardinal for the survival of performance as an art form. From time immemorial this magical interface, based on human contact between the performer/s and viewer/s in a pre-determined time and space, has been celebrated. Given the changing context of performance in 21st century, expectations of audiences have dramatically changed, challenging dancers to re-think dynamics of their art. Entry of dance in media, combined with unprecedented dance boom in myriad forms, calls for evolving professional support systems in India that mediate between dance and its audience.

TEDxDharamsala: Vision and Background

TEDxDharamsala aims to to stimulate thought and spread ideas in this beautiful multi-cultural town of Dharamsala in Himachal Pradesh, India.

Dharamsala has had a rich tradition with a confluence of people from across the world and the serene nature of the place makes it apt for an event like TEDx.

In this age of change, TEDxDharamsala aspires to create an environment which evokes fresh thought and leads to positive action. We look forward to collaborating with universal citizens and especially people from Himachal, in spreading sparks of wisdom wide and far.

Each year, TEDxDharamsala will host an event focused on a relevant theme for our times. The event will foster conversation in this diverse land and move towards actionable steps for a positive and sustainable future.

What is TEDx

In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TED has created a program called TEDx. TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. Our event is called TEDxDharamsala, where x = independently organized TED event. At TEDxDharamsala event, TEDTalks video and live speakers will combine to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events, including ours, are self-organized.
